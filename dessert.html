The Prytaneum

Would the Prytaneum have been a proper punishment for Socrates?

His request for a “penalty” for his “crimes” (he doesn’t accept the latter so the former makes no sense) is that he should receive free room and board at the Prytaneum, where the most celebrated victors in the Olympics stay. Since Socrates makes the Athenians really happy, whereas the Olympic victors give them a superfi cial happiness, the “punishment” would fi t the “crime.” P. 30
