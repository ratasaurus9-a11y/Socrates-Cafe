The Phaedo

What happens after death?

"In the Phaedo, just an hour before his death, Socrates says, “Philosophy is nothing but the preparation for death and dying.”13 (“No way to recruit majors,” my former professor Johanna Meehan used to say.) Among other things, this statement means that philosophers must confront the fact of their deaths and, by implication, the fact of their lives; for life and death are stages of one underlying cycle." P. 27
